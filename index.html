Diet Tips and Reccomendations

When it comes to leading a healthy lifestyle, diet is crucial; especially if you are exercising and need to recover after workouts. I don't mean counting calories or strict dieting, I am referring to what goes into your body. Here I cannot give specifics, as everyone's body is different, but there are some general guidelines. It is important to eliminate processed foods when possible, eat when you are hungry and stop when you are full, listen to how your body reacts to different foods, and make sure you are eating enough. Every function in your body is affected by what you eat, so treat food like fuel for your body.


Tips:

  1. Eat a small portion of a carbohydrate dense snack shortly before exercising to give your body easily digestible calori es to easily expend while working out.

  2. Wait for a bit after eating a meal to let your food digest and decide if you need to eat more.

Gear

For beginners, finding the right pair of shoes is very important, as the wrong shoes can cause injury. There are different shoes for running roads vs trails. Trail shoes tend to have more padding whereas road running shoes are lighter. When buying a pair of shoes, you should see if an employee can assess your running form to determine which shoes would be the best fit for you. I cannot give specific advice on running shoes because the best type is completely dependent on the shape pf your foot and your running form. I can say though that most times, going to your local running store can make a world of a difference as opposed to going to a chain store.

Races

When choosing a race, you should first decide whether you would like to run a trail race or a road race. Note that trails are easier on the joints, but you can often go faster on roads because you don't have to deal with rougher terrain. There are also flat races and hilly races. If you want to run hilly races, you should emphasize hill repeats in training. Trail races can also get pretty crowded on the trail, so one thing to consider is having to pass people in tight spaces. Trail races are also heavily impacted by weather, road races not as much, since roads don't get muddy or slippery. Running trails however can be beautiful and often times you are more hidden from the crowd as opposed to a road race where typically people are watching during the whole race.

Important Tips

  • Try running both roads and trails first to see which is preferable.
  • On rest days, you can do workouts that are easy on the joints, like swimming or biking.
  • Consider finding a local running group to train with. For some people it can be preferable to run with others.
  • Before each run you should warm up by slowly jogging for 5-10 minutes. After each run you should cool down by doing the same.
  • STRETCH AFTER YOU RUN. I cannot emphasize this enough. You can find simple stretches online. Not stretching WILL lead to an injury.
  • Hydrate plenty, but don't wait until you are very dehydrated and then chug a ton of water. This does not work the same as drinking water steadily throughout the day.
  • The night before a race it's best to eat a light meal, and the morning before a race it's best to eat a light meal of mainly carbohydrates.
  • Do a 10 minute VERY slow warmup about 15-20 minutes before your race.
  • Stretch as needed, about 5 minutes before your race.
